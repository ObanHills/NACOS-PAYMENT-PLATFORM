NACOS Code of Ethics

Principles That Guide Computing Students

As future technologists, we are bound by a shared responsibility to build, use, and advocate for technology that is ethical, inclusive, and beneficial to society.

Integrity in Technology

Computing professionals must act with honesty and integrity in all technical work. Writing clean, honest code, citing sources, and never misrepresenting capabilities are foundational to a trustworthy tech ecosystem.

ACM Code of Ethics · Principle 1

Inclusive Design

Technology must serve everyone. Computing students are called to design systems that are accessible, culturally sensitive, and free from bias — ensuring no group is excluded or harmed by the software we build.

Digital Inclusion · IEEE Ethics

Privacy & Data Protection

Respect for personal data is non-negotiable. Every system we build must collect only what is necessary, protect it rigorously, and give users meaningful control over their own information.

GDPR Principles · Data Ethics

Fairness & Algorithmic Justice

Algorithms shape lives. We must audit our systems for bias, ensure fair outcomes across demographics, and refuse to build tools that discriminate — whether intentionally or through negligent design.

Responsible AI · Fairness in ML

Technology for Public Good

The highest purpose of computing is to improve human life. NACOS members are encouraged to apply their skills to solve real community problems — from healthcare to education to governance.

NACOS Mission · SDG Goals

Transparency & Accountability

Systems that affect people must be explainable. Computing professionals should document their work, disclose limitations, and take responsibility for the consequences of the technology they deploy.

Explainable AI · Open Source Ethics

Sustainable Computing

Technology has an environmental footprint. Efficient code, responsible hardware choices, and awareness of energy consumption are part of the ethical responsibility of every computing professional.

Green Computing · UN SDG 13

Continuous Learning

The tech landscape evolves rapidly. Ethical computing requires staying informed — about new threats, emerging standards, and the societal impact of technologies like AI, blockchain, and biometrics.

Professional Development · Lifelong Learning
Career Pathways

Where Computing Takes You

A computing degree opens doors to some of the most impactful, well-paid, and rapidly growing careers in the world. Explore the major paths available to NACOS members.

Software Engineering

Very High Demand

The backbone of the digital world. Software engineers design, build, test, and maintain the applications and systems that power everything from mobile apps to enterprise platforms.

What You'll Do

Write and review code, architect systems, collaborate with product teams, debug production issues, and continuously ship features. You'll work across the full stack or specialise in frontend, backend, mobile, or embedded systems.

Key Skills to Build
  • Data structures & algorithms
  • Version control (Git)
  • At least one language deeply (Python, Java, JavaScript, C++)
  • System design & architecture
  • Testing & CI/CD pipelines
Career Progression

Junior Developer → Mid-level Engineer → Senior Engineer → Staff/Principal Engineer → Engineering Manager / CTO

Top Employers in Nigeria & Globally

Flutterwave, Paystack, Andela, Google, Microsoft, Meta, Amazon, startups across fintech, healthtech, and edtech.

₦2M–₦15M+Annual salary range (Nigeria)
$80K–$200K+Global salary range
25%Job growth by 2032 (BLS)

Cybersecurity

Critical Shortage

Defenders of the digital frontier. Cybersecurity professionals protect organisations from data breaches, ransomware, and nation-state attacks in an increasingly hostile threat landscape.

What You'll Do

Conduct penetration testing, monitor security operations centres (SOC), respond to incidents, design secure architectures, perform vulnerability assessments, and train staff on security awareness.

Key Skills to Build
  • Networking fundamentals (TCP/IP, DNS, firewalls)
  • Linux & command-line proficiency
  • Ethical hacking & penetration testing
  • SIEM tools (Splunk, IBM QRadar)
  • Cryptography & PKI
Key Certifications

CompTIA Security+, CEH (Certified Ethical Hacker), OSCP, CISSP, CISM — these significantly boost employability and salary.

Specialisations

Penetration Tester, SOC Analyst, Cloud Security Engineer, Digital Forensics Analyst, Malware Analyst, GRC (Governance, Risk & Compliance) Specialist.

3.5MGlobal unfilled positions (2025)
$95K–$180KGlobal salary range
32%Job growth by 2032 (BLS)

Data Science & AI

Very High Demand

The science of turning raw data into decisions. Data scientists and AI engineers build the models that power recommendation engines, fraud detection, medical diagnosis, and autonomous systems.

What You'll Do

Collect and clean datasets, build and train machine learning models, deploy AI systems to production, interpret results for business stakeholders, and research new algorithms and architectures.

Key Skills to Build
  • Python (NumPy, Pandas, Scikit-learn, PyTorch/TensorFlow)
  • Statistics & probability
  • SQL & data wrangling
  • Machine learning fundamentals
  • Data visualisation (Matplotlib, Tableau, Power BI)
Specialisations

Machine Learning Engineer, NLP Engineer, Computer Vision Engineer, Data Analyst, Business Intelligence Developer, AI Research Scientist, MLOps Engineer.

Nigeria Opportunity

Banks, telecoms (MTN, Airtel), and government agencies are actively hiring data professionals. The Central Bank of Nigeria's digital finance push has created significant demand for data-driven roles.

#1Fastest growing job (LinkedIn 2024)
$100K–$220KGlobal salary range
36%Job growth by 2033 (BLS)

Cloud Computing & DevOps

Very High Demand

The infrastructure layer of the modern internet. Cloud and DevOps engineers build, automate, and scale the systems that keep applications running reliably for millions of users.

What You'll Do

Provision cloud infrastructure, automate deployments with CI/CD pipelines, manage containerised workloads, monitor system health, optimise costs, and ensure high availability and disaster recovery.

Key Skills to Build
  • AWS, Azure, or Google Cloud Platform
  • Docker & Kubernetes
  • Infrastructure as Code (Terraform, Ansible)
  • Linux administration
  • CI/CD (GitHub Actions, Jenkins)
Key Certifications

AWS Solutions Architect, Google Cloud Professional, Azure Administrator, Certified Kubernetes Administrator (CKA) — each certification can add $10K–$30K to your salary.

Specialisations

Cloud Architect, Site Reliability Engineer (SRE), Platform Engineer, DevSecOps Engineer, FinOps Analyst.

$110K–$200KGlobal salary range
28%Job growth by 2032
$679BGlobal cloud market 2024

Product Management

Growing Fast

The bridge between technology and business. Product managers define what gets built, why, and for whom — translating user needs and business goals into product roadmaps that engineering teams execute.

What You'll Do

Conduct user research, write product requirements, prioritise features, work with designers and engineers, analyse metrics, run A/B tests, and communicate strategy to stakeholders and executives.

Key Skills to Build
  • User research & empathy mapping
  • Data analysis (SQL, Google Analytics)
  • Agile & Scrum methodologies
  • Wireframing (Figma, Balsamiq)
  • Stakeholder communication & storytelling
Why Computing Students Excel Here

Technical literacy gives computing graduates a massive edge — you can speak the language of engineers, evaluate feasibility, and earn the trust of development teams in ways non-technical PMs cannot.

Career Progression

Associate PM → Product Manager → Senior PM → Group PM → VP of Product → Chief Product Officer (CPO)

$95K–$185KGlobal salary range
₦3M–₦20MNigeria salary range
19%Job growth by 2032

UI/UX Design

Growing Fast

The art and science of human-computer interaction. UX designers ensure that digital products are intuitive, accessible, and delightful — turning complex systems into seamless experiences.

What You'll Do

Conduct usability research, create user personas and journey maps, design wireframes and prototypes, run usability tests, collaborate with developers on implementation, and iterate based on user feedback.

Key Skills to Build
  • Figma (industry standard design tool)
  • User research methods (interviews, surveys, usability testing)
  • Information architecture
  • Accessibility standards (WCAG)
  • Basic HTML/CSS (to communicate with developers)
Specialisations

UX Researcher, UI Designer, Interaction Designer, Design Systems Engineer, Motion Designer, Accessibility Specialist, Voice/Conversational UI Designer.

Portfolio is Everything

Unlike most tech roles, UX hiring is portfolio-driven. Start building case studies now — redesign existing apps, solve real problems in your community, and document your process thoroughly.

$75K–$160KGlobal salary range
23%Job growth by 2032
$1.1TROI of good UX (Forrester)

Database Engineering

Stable & Essential

Every organisation runs on data. Database engineers and administrators design, optimise, and protect the data stores that power banking systems, hospitals, e-commerce platforms, and government services.

What You'll Do

Design database schemas, write complex queries, optimise slow queries, manage backups and recovery, implement replication and sharding, enforce data security, and migrate legacy systems.

Key Skills to Build
  • SQL (PostgreSQL, MySQL, Oracle)
  • NoSQL (MongoDB, Redis, Cassandra)
  • Query optimisation & indexing
  • Data modelling & normalisation
  • Backup, replication & disaster recovery
Modern Evolution

The role is evolving into Data Engineering — building ETL pipelines, data lakes, and real-time streaming systems using tools like Apache Kafka, Spark, dbt, and Snowflake.

$85K–$160KGlobal salary range
9%Job growth by 2032
Every orgNeeds data management

Blockchain & Web3

Emerging

The decentralised internet. Blockchain developers build trustless systems — from DeFi protocols and NFT platforms to supply chain solutions and digital identity systems that don't rely on central authorities.

What You'll Do

Write and audit smart contracts, build decentralised applications (dApps), integrate wallets and blockchain APIs, design tokenomics, and contribute to open-source Web3 protocols.

Key Skills to Build
  • Solidity (Ethereum smart contracts)
  • Web3.js / Ethers.js
  • Cryptography fundamentals
  • Hardhat / Foundry (development frameworks)
  • DeFi protocols & tokenomics
Nigeria Context

Nigeria is one of the highest crypto-adopting nations globally. Local projects like Korapay, Busha, and Yellow Card are building blockchain-adjacent infrastructure. The CBN's eNaira is also creating demand for blockchain expertise in government.
